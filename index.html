Hi, I'm Miheer Dewaskar

I am a postdoc in the Department of Statistical Science at Duke University, working with David Dunson on the foundations of robust and interpretable Bayesian inference. I obtained my PhD in Statistics and Operations Research from UNC Chapel Hill in May 2021, under the direction of Shankar Bhamidi, Amarjit Budhiraja, and Andrew Nobel.

My research in probability and theoretical statistics

My research interest include correlation mining, density based clustering, non-parametric estimation, asymptotics of model-based inference under misspecification, concentration inequalities, stochastic processes, and large deviation theory.
